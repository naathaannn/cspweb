In AP Computer Science Principles, the Cybersecurity unit focuses on safeguarding data and devices by understanding the mechanisms behind online data security. This unit covers the importance of protecting personal identifiable information (PII), tracking how user data is monitored, and recognizing various cyber attacks. Students learn about vulnerabilities exploited through phishing, rogue access points, and malware, while also exploring defensive measures such as data encryption, secure Internet protocols, digital certificates, and robust user authentication methods. The unit emphasizes the balance between functionality and privacy, providing insight into geolocation tracking and user history, and highlighting the ethical responsibilities inherent in managing digital information.

  • Introduction to Online Data Security: Gain foundational knowledge on protecting data and devices from cyber threats and understanding safe online practices.
  • PII (Personally Identifiable Information): Understand what constitutes PII, how it is tracked online, and the critical importance of protecting this sensitive data.
  • User Data Tracking & User History: Explore methods of online tracking, the implications of data collection, and the privacy issues that arise from monitoring user behavior.
  • Geolocation: Learn how geolocation technologies work, and discuss the trade-offs between useful services and potential privacy invasions.
  • Cyber Attacks: Investigate various types of cyber attacks, including phishing schemes, rogue access points, and malware, and understand how these threats compromise security.
  • Data Encryption Techniques: Study how encryption protects data integrity and confidentiality during transmission and storage.
  • Secure Internet Protocols: Examine protocols such as HTTPS that ensure safe and encrypted online communications.
  • Digital Certificates: Understand the role of digital certificates in authenticating websites and securing data exchanges.
  • User Authentication Methods: Learn about different authentication techniques, including passwords, biometrics, and multi-factor authentication, to verify user identity and prevent unauthorized access.
