App design

Content

In Code.org’s AP Computer Science Principles, the App Design unit focuses on creating interactive applications that are both functional and user-friendly. This unit teaches students to employ design thinking to plan, prototype, and refine their app ideas by considering key elements such as user interface design, usability, and overall functionality. Students learn to sketch out wireframes, develop interactive prototypes, and iteratively debug their projects, all while considering ethical and accessibility issues to ensure that their apps meet diverse user needs.

  • Design Thinking: Emphasize a creative, iterative process to define problems, brainstorm solutions, and refine app concepts.
  • User Interface (UI) Design: Focus on creating intuitive layouts, navigation, and interactive elements that enhance usability.
  • Prototyping and Wireframing: Develop visual representations and interactive models of app ideas to test and improve functionality.
  • Iteration and Debugging: Continuously test, refine, and debug the app to enhance performance and user experience.
  • Accessibility and Ethics: Incorporate inclusive design practices and consider ethical implications to ensure apps are accessible and respectful of user privacy.
